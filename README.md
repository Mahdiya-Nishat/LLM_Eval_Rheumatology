# LLM_Eval_Rheumatology

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) represents a chronic autoimmune challenge, that requires lifelong immunosuppressive therapy and constant monitoring for systemic joint damage. Effective management demands intensive patient education alongside personalized guidance to prevent irreversible destruction of critical skeletal structures. However, a global shortage of rheumatologists constrains access to timely counseling with routine clinical encounters. Henceforth, patients are increasingly turning to publicly accessible large language models (LLMs) for medical advice, due to their ability to provide immediate guidance without barriers of time, cost, and clinical availability. Despite such accessibility, the reliability of such models remains uncertain, particularly with respect to hallucinations and lack of actionable guidance, necessitating their assessment in patient-facing clinical contexts of RA. To address this, we conducted a blinded evaluation of the five most commonly used LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, and DeepSeek) using 15 patient-derived RA questions under zero-shot conditions. Responses were independently assessed by three practicing rheumatologists across five clinically relevant domains: factual accuracy, completeness, safety and harm avoidance, readability and clarity, and actionability. Findings suggest that LLMs can serve as valuable supervised adjuncts to bridge the existing healthcare workforce gaps. However, persistent limitations in depth and nuance preclude their use as autonomous clinical decision-support tools.
